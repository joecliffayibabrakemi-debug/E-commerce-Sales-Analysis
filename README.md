# E-commerce-Sales-Analysis
**Exploratory Data Analysis of E-Commerce Sales &amp; Brand Equity (Excel Project)**
[E-commerce.Project-Joecliff Wilson.xlsx](https://github.com/user-attachments/files/26659235/E-commerce.Project-Joecliff.Wilson.xlsx)


**Executive Summary**

This project analyzes e-commerce sales data and brand performance across major platforms **(Amazon, Jumia, Konga, and Temu)** using **Microsoft Excel**. The goal was to evaluate how pricing strategies, discount levels, and customer engagement (ratings and reviews) influence platform and brand performance.

Due to the lack of a ready-made dataset, data was independently researched, collected, and structured from multiple e-commerce platforms, then analyzed in Excel.

The analysis reveals a strong imbalance in customer engagement, with Amazon contributing approximately **92.68%** of total reviews, despite not being a local platform. Interestingly, Amazon and Temu (international platforms with longer delivery times) still command higher customer trust compared to Jumia and Konga (local platforms).

This suggests that trust, brand perception, and perceived product quality outweigh delivery speed in influencing customer engagement.

**The Business Problem**

E-commerce businesses must balance pricing, delivery efficiency, and customer trust to remain competitive.

However, key challenges include:

-Over-reliance on discounts without clear impact on engagement

-Lower customer trust in local platforms (Jumia, Konga)

-Uneven distribution of reviews across platforms

-Difficulty competing with international platforms despite faster delivery

**A critical concern highlighted in this analysis is:**

**Customers prefer international platforms (Amazon, Temu) over local ones, even with longer delivery times.**

**Without addressing this, businesses risk:**

-Losing market share to international competitors

-Weak brand credibility

-Inefficient pricing and marketing strategies

**Result and Business Recommendation**

**Key Findings**

- Amazon dominates engagement with ~92.68% of total reviews

- Temu offers the highest average discount (~53%), yet does not match Amazon in engagement

- Jumia and Konga (local platforms) show significantly lower customer engagement

- Customers trust international platforms more, despite longer delivery timelines

- Discounts alone do not guarantee engagement — trust and brand perception are stronger drivers


**Business Recommendations**

1. Build Customer Trust on Local Platforms

- Improve product authenticity verification
- Strengthen return/refund policies
- Highlight customer reviews and ratings more prominently

2. Rethink Discount Strategy

- Avoid excessive discounting as a primary growth strategy
- Focus on value-driven pricing instead

3. Improve Customer Experience

- Enhance UI/UX and service reliability
- Communicate delivery expectations clearly

4. Leverage Social Proof

- Encourage verified reviews to boost credibility
- Partner with trusted brands to improve perception
